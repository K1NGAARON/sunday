How To Preboard Employees

You'll hear about the phase ‘preboarding’ a lot in HR. And it's okay if you're not fully sure what it means. What do recent hires expect from their new employer? How often should you interact with them? What are the proper tasks or information?

The priority should be on prepping your new employee and creating exciting expectations as their start date draws near. Discover how to preboard your employees in the best way.

Preboarding helps employees feel welcomed and prepared for the day ahead. It also makes them more productive and less likely to leave early. Essentially, it is the very first step of the overall onboarding process. And yet a step that is overlooked by so many HR-managers and business owners.

Give Them A Warm Welcome

Pre-boarding should reassure your new employee that though they may have been swimming in a sea of choice, choosing your company was the right one.

It’s easy to forget how much an employee’s first impression matters. If they walk into a cold, dark, or dirty space, they might not feel as welcome as they would if they walked into a welcoming environment.

Start communicating how excited you are for them to join, prepare them for success on day one and share your corporate culture from step one.

Set Expectations

You should also make sure your employees understand what to expect before they start working. This includes things like dress code, office layout, and any other policies or procedures that affect their job performance.

You might be surprised to learn that candidates want to know what is expected of them before they arrive at work on the first day. And while some want to know so they can wow you at the Monday morning meeting, the majority merely want to avoid looking foolish, going to the wrong office, or getting the dress code wrong.

Create an Environment That Makes Them Feel Comfortable

A preboarding checklist should include things like making sure the office has been cleaned, having coffee available, and ensuring there are no outstanding issues with any equipment.

8 things that should be included in preboarding:

  • Announce new hires within the team
  • Introduce a buddy
  • Setup their desk, email and software
  • Stay in touch before they start
  • Invite them to a company event
  • Share your company values, perks and policies
  • Send a first day email
  • Prepare an onboarding box

Create an Environment That Encourages Productivity

It’s not enough to just give your employees a warm welcome; you need to provide them with a productive environment where they can do their jobs well. A good preboarding process will help ensure that your employees are prepared to perform their duties effectively.

By employing preboarding into your recruitment and onboarding strategy, you help keep up the momentum and excitement established in the hiring process. Not only does it increase new hires’ excitement, but it also fosters a relationship with them before orientation.
