First things first?

Pre-Boarding! The onboarding of your new hire starts the moment they sign the contract. So pre-boarding is the period of time between an employee accepting their job offer and their first day. Essentially, it is the very first step of the overall onboarding process. And yet a step that is overlooked by so many HR-managers and business owners.

Pre-boarding should reassure your new employee that though they may have been swimming in a sea of choice, choosing your company was the right one. Start communicating how excited you are for them to join, prepare them for success on day one and share your corporate culture from step one.

By employing preboarding into your recruitment and onboarding strategy, you help keep up the momentum and excitement established in the hiring process. Not only does it increase new hires’ excitement, but it also fosters a relationship with them before orientation. So before your new employees step foot into your office, do your homework with the following checklist. This list will help you identify every way to make them feel valued and welcome.

What’s an onboarding box?

When a new employee joins your firm, you want to make a lasting impression on them with a gift that embodies your organization's culture and values. Onboarding boxes are a creative approach to make new employees feel special. During the pandemic onboarding boxes were the only personal touchpoint they had with the company they were about to start working for. The onboarding box is set to become a feature of many forward-thinking HR departments as individuals return to work in the office.

Onboarding boxes are usually filled with a range of branded items like apparel, pens, notebooks and water bottles. The kit you create should convey the culture of your organization and instill a sense of pride and loyalty among incoming employees. Since they are built to transform new hires into real brand ambassadors, the company also benefits from employees who become more quickly productive team players. Because you make them feel at ease in their workplace, a part of the team, and confident in their ability to accomplish their duties from day one.

What should be included in an onboarding box?

The ideal onboarding box is different for every company. Depending on your DNA, the best welcome pack for new employees is customized to represent the company core values, inspiring the new employee in their first week. Not only the items in the box should be an extension of your brand identity, also the box itself can be fully personalized. This way, a total experience is created, one that your stakeholders will certainly not miss out on.

The items that should be in the box are a mix of products that a new employee could make use of at work, along with objects that are slightly more personal and could be used by someone outside of their workplace. Think about office supplies, hoodies, coffee mugs, notepads, tote bags, towels, and even sportswear.

In order for company fashion to be worn outside and therefore increase the brand’s visibility, companies should keep in mind that such garments must be tasteful and of high quality. Your unique culture translated in fashion is the ultimate way to activate your brand ambassadors. Add the clever copywriting and eye catching design, and this is an onboarding box that really makes the recipient feel welcome.
